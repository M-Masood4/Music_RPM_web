How We Track and Distribute Revenue from YouTube Shorts

YouTube’s Revenue Sharing Policy

YouTube Shorts operates under a 45/55 revenue-sharing model. YouTube retains 45% of ad revenue generated from Shorts, while the remaining 55% is distributed among eligible creators, including music rights holders like those using our music app.

How Our Music App Receives Revenue Information

YouTube’s Content ID System

YouTube uses a system called Content ID to identify music and other copyrighted material used in Shorts. When a user uploads a Short that contains a track from our music app's library, YouTube automatically flags the video using Content ID.

Monthly Reports from YouTube

Every month, YouTube sends us detailed reports about the ad revenue generated from Shorts. These reports contain valuable metrics such as:

  • The number of views for Shorts containing licensed music from our library.
  • The revenue generated by those views.
  • The portion of revenue that belongs to the music rights holders (our music app).

How We Identify Users and Allocate Revenue

Tracking Channels and Usage

To ensure that revenue is accurately tracked, our music app monitors which channels are using our music. This is typically done by:

  • Analyzing the Content ID reports from YouTube.
  • Using dashboards provided by YouTube to monitor specific channels and videos using our tracks.

Distribution to Users

If we share a percentage of the revenue with users:

  • We track specific Shorts that use your music through Content ID.
  • Based on the agreement with each creator, we allocate the respective share of revenue to you, determined by the number of views and ad revenue generated by your Short.

Automation and Revenue Tracking

Our platform uses APIs and automated systems to pull revenue and view data directly from YouTube, update your user dashboard, and automatically calculate and allocate payments based on a predefined revenue-sharing formula.

Revenue Notification and Payout

Once your total revenue is calculated and allocated, we will notify you via your dashboard. When your earnings reach a certain threshold (for example, $100), the payout method will be activated, allowing you to receive your share of the earnings directly.
